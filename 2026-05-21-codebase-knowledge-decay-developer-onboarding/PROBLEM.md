## Codebase Knowledge Decay & Developer Onboarding Intelligence

**Who experiences this problem:** Software engineering teams at growing companies — particularly those with high developer turnover or rapidly expanding codebases — who lose critical architectural context, decision rationale, and system-specific knowledge every time an engineer departs or a new one joins.

**How frequently:** 
Organizations lose an average of 42% of project-specific knowledge when turnover exceeds 20% per year, and each developer turnover can set a team back 4–8 weeks in delivery time, with developers losing approximately 10 hours a week just trying to source basic information they need to do their jobs.


**Why current solutions are insufficient:** 
Traditional documentation explains what the code does but rarely explains *why* specific architectural decisions were made, how services interact, or what trade-offs shaped the system's design — meaning developers may understand the code yet still struggle to contribute effectively.
 
Poor documentation is one of the top causes of onboarding delays, and it can increase onboarding time by 2–4 weeks
 — yet no dominant commercial product automatically captures and resurfaces tacit architectural knowledge in real-time as code evolves.

**Why software can solve this:** 
AI-powered documentation allows engineers to query the system directly and retrieve contextual explanations without manually searching through code, reducing the need for repeated explanations from senior engineers — cutting onboarding time that previously took about seven days down to one or two days.
 A software tool can continuously mine git history, PR discussions, Slack threads, and architectural records to build and maintain a living, queryable knowledge graph tied directly to the codebase.

**Estimated impact if solved:** 
Companies that invest in structured knowledge transfer typically achieve 20–35% faster productivity ramp-up.
 Across an industry where 
developers are already losing 23% of their time to technical debt and fragmented information, eliminating the knowledge-decay bottleneck would translate to substantial productivity gains, lower attrition, and measurably faster feature delivery at scale.
